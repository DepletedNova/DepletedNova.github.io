Bound by Time

Joseph Davis

"Am I capable of creating an abstract puzzle game?"

I am chose this question in particular because it challenges my skillset and my ability to think outside of the normal bounds of logic. The goal of the project is, of course, to make an abstract puzzle game. What has this consisted of so far? The game is a story-based game wrapped around the concept of time and the interpersonal thoughts of the player.

The timeline is a complete disaster to put it simply: I had been expecting to have made some sort of Demo by this current stage and I can not say that I have anything that is playable. A few challenges to be noted to causing this include a balanced schedule: school and this large project need their own seperate times as they are on two different scales. Another is the general mental toll that creating a game on your own can create: as this is a larger project it is a bit of a more daunting task to undergo which can delay motivation to continue work on it. I have devised a few ways to manage these and it's to simply create a schedule; something to keep my time organized into bits that I can manage to work through.

Now am I capable of creating an abstract puzzle game? I think the answer is a simple no. Altough I do have the capability to do such I do not think I have the capacity or endurance. I have great experience in programming and it is most likely the activity I spend the most time on. This experience does not quite scale up to doing a very daunting project on your own as that does require a higher mental capacity to fully flesh out and create (which is why games usually have a team of three or more). I am more capable of smaller side-projects in which that I can constantly divert focus into new things rather than the same monotonous task. I will not be halting progress on the game but will continue trying my best but I do not think that this is a capability of mine.
